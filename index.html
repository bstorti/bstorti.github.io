About me

I am a Mechanical Engineer graduated from the National Technological University (UTN), Santa Fe, Argentina, and currently completing a PhD in Computational Mechanics at the CIMEC institute.

"My work sits at the intersection of fluid physics, numerical methods, and high-performance computing."

My research journey started in the Flowmeter Laboratory during my third undergraduate year, where I contributed to the aerodynamic design of a solar car for the Atacama Solar Challenge in Chile using CFD simulations. This grew into my degree thesis on the aerodynamic optimisation of an urban-scale Horizontal Axis Wind Turbine (HAWT).

Alongside my research, I have taught SolidWorks Motion for both industrial and academic audiences, and contributed to the full design and construction of a pneumatic olive harvester — one of the projects I'm proudest of for the breadth of engineering challenges it involved.

My PhD research focuses on particle methods, immersed-boundary techniques, and a Chimera overlapping-mesh algorithm for complex fluid-structure interaction problems, using Code Saturne and PETSc-FEM on HPC clusters.
