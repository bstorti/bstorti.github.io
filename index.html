About me

I am a Mechanical Engineer graduated from the National Technological University (UTN), Santa Fe, Argentina, currently completing a PhD in Computational Mechanics at the CIMEC institute.

My work sits at the intersection of fluid physics, numerical methods, and high-performance computing.

My research journey began in the Flowmeter Laboratory during my third undergraduate year, where I contributed to the aerodynamic design of a solar car for the Atacama Solar Challenge in Chile. This grew into my degree thesis on the aerodynamic optimisation of an urban-scale Horizontal Axis Wind Turbine (HAWT).

Alongside my research I have taught SolidWorks Motion for both industrial and academic audiences, and contributed to the full design and construction of a pneumatic olive harvester — one of the projects I am proudest of for the breadth of engineering challenges it required.

My PhD research focuses on particle methods, immersed-boundary techniques, and a Chimera overlapping-mesh algorithm for complex fluid-structure interaction problems, running on HPC clusters with Code Saturne and PETSc-FEM.
