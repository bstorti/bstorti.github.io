Aerodynamic design of a Solar car

I am Mechanical Engineer graduated from the National Technological University from Santa Fe, Argentina. Currently i'm doing a PhD on engineering, specifically in Computational Mechanics, at the CIMEC institute located in Santa Fe as well. In the third year of the engineering career I applied for a scholarship to work in the Flowmeter Laboratory of the University. We started working on the aerodinamic design of a solar car using CFD (Computational Fluid Dynamics) to compete in the Atacama Solar Challenge, in Chile. Afterwards, in conjuction with my laboratory colleagues and our Director, we started working on the aerodinamics optimization of a urban medium-scale Horizontal Axis Wind Turbine, which would later become our degree thesis. Meanwhile I was studying engineering, I've started working as a teacher of the software "SolidWorks Motion" both for the industry and academic enviroments. Also, during my career I've been involved on several engineering projects. Between them one specifically was very important for my professional training, because of all the design, calculation and computations that the project involved. This project was about the entire design and construction of a new type of olives harvester based on pulses of air avoiding any damage on the olives trees (more information about this project in the Portfolio section).
