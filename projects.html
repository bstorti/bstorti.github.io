2023 — 2025

MATCH II: Advanced Thermoplastic Composites

Postdoctoral ResearchNantes UniversitéHPC

Radiative–conductive 2D & 3D microscale thermal FEM modeling with experimental validation for the Automated Fiber Placement (AFP) process.

Abstract & Details

During my postdoctoral fellowship at Nantes Université, funded by the Institut de Recherche Technologique (IRT) Jules Verne, I worked on the Manufacturing of Advanced Thermoplastic Composites with High Thickness II (MATCH II) project. My main tasks included adapting a radiative heat transfer FEM code for the AFP process in an HPC environment, creating 2D macroscale thermal modeling of a lab-scale setup, and developing a 3D multiscale Eulerian FEM framework for in-situ consolidation modeling. I also implemented an ALE+Active–Inactive elements approach to accurately simulate roller displacement.

2021 — 2023

SAMFAST: Additive Manufacturing of Molds

Postdoctoral ResearchTopology Opt.Injection Molding

High-fidelity 3D FEM modeling of heat transfer and 3D cooling channel optimization using a stochastic approach and overset mesh FEM strategy.

Abstract & Details

Funded by the Banque Publique d'Investissement (BPI) of France, the SAMFAST project focused on the rapid additive manufacturing of plastic injection mold inserts. My research centered on 1D transient heat transfer analysis to compare cooling times in steel and thermoplastic molds. I utilized an overset mesh FEM strategy to entirely eliminate the need for remeshing during the optimization phase, and performed multi-material topology optimization to significantly enhance thermal performance.

2015 — 2020

Chimera Overlapping-Mesh Algorithm

PhD ThesisFSIHPC

Development of an overlapping-mesh technique for simulating fluid-structure interaction on complex moving geometries running on Linux-based HPC clusters.

Abstract & Details

This doctoral research focused on particle methods, immersed-boundary techniques, and a Chimera overlapping-mesh algorithm for complex fluid-structure interaction (FSI) problems. The methodology allows for large relative displacements between solid bodies without the need for computationally expensive mesh-updating or remeshing procedures. The algorithms were parallelized and run on High-Performance Computing (HPC) environments utilizing Code Saturne and PETSc-FEM.

2017 — 2019

VAWT Deflector Optimisation

Wind EnergyShape OptimisationNeural Networks

Numerical shape optimisation of deflectors for a Vertical Axis Wind Turbine using adjoint methods and neural networks.

Abstract & Details

To improve the efficiency of a Savonius wind turbine, we designed a set of deflector plates using a metamodel-based optimization approach. The project utilized Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) combined with Artificial Neural Networks to rapidly evaluate the aerodynamic performance of thousands of potential deflector shapes, ultimately yielding a configuration that significantly increased the power coefficient.
